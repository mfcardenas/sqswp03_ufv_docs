Four Phases of Human-Centered Design

Follow the systematic HCD process defined in ISO 9241-210, the international standard for human-centered design of interactive systems.

1

Understand & Specify Context

Identify users, tasks, equipment, and physical/social environments. Apply persona development and context analysis methods from Lab 1.

2

Specify User Requirements

Define explicit user and organizational requirements based on context analysis. Prioritize requirements using systematic methods.

3

Produce Design Solutions

Create design solutions that meet requirements. Apply dialogue principles from Lab 2 and iterate based on evaluation feedback.

4

Evaluate Against Requirements

Systematically evaluate designs using heuristic evaluation (Lab 3) and usability testing (Lab 5) methods.
